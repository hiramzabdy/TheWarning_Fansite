About

From Monterrey to the World

The Warning is a Mexican hard rock band formed in 2013 in Monterrey, Nuevo León, by the Villarreal Vélez sisters. What started as a childhood passion fueled by the video game Rock Band grew into a global touring act with millions of fans across the world.

The Beginning

The sisters all received classical piano training from a young age. After years playing Rock Band at home, Daniela chose guitar, Paulina chose drums, and seven-year-old Alejandra picked up the bass. In 2013, the power trio was born.

In 2014, their YouTube cover of Metallica's "Enter Sandman" went viral, amassing over 26 million views. Metallica guitarist Kirk Hammett himself praised Paulina: "The drummer kicks maximum ass!"

The Rise

After appearing on The Ellen DeGeneres Show in 2015 and earning scholarships to Berklee College of Music, the band built a fiercely loyal fanbase. They signed with Lava / Republic Records in 2020 and have since released four studio albums, toured with Muse, Foo Fighters, and Guns N' Roses, and earned a Latin Grammy nomination.

In 2025, the band sold out three nights at Mexico City's 10,000-capacity Auditorio Nacional — a milestone that led to their first concert film, Live from Auditorio Nacional, CDMX.

The Sisters

Meet the Villarreal Vélez Trio

Three siblings who share blood, a stage, and an unbreakable musical chemistry.

D

Daniela "Dany"

Lead Vocals · Guitar · Piano

The eldest sister and frontwoman. Her guitar style is heavily inspired by Matt Bellamy of Muse. She writes much of the band's lyrics and commands the stage with fiery energy.

P

Paulina "Pau"

Drums · Vocals · Piano

The powerhouse behind the kit. Profiled in Tom Tom Magazine at age 12. She's the band's creative director, co-lead vocalist, and a force of nature — both behind the drums and on the mic.

A

Alejandra "Ale"

Bass · Backing Vocals · Piano

The youngest sister anchors the rhythm section with a deep, melodic bass style. She also provides ethereal backing harmonies that round out the band's vocal texture.

History

Timeline of a Rising Force

2013
The Band Forms

The Villarreal Vélez sisters form a power trio in Monterrey, Mexico. Daniela (guitar), Paulina (drums), and Alejandra (bass) begin posting covers on YouTube.

2014
"Enter Sandman" Goes Viral

Their cover of Metallica's "Enter Sandman" explodes on YouTube, reaching over 26 million views. Kirk Hammett calls Paulina's drumming "maximum ass." Paulina is profiled in Tom Tom Magazine.

2015
Ellen & Berklee

The band appears on The Ellen DeGeneres Show. With donations from Ellen and Target, the sisters attend a five-week program at Berklee College of Music. They release debut EP Escape the Mind.

2017
XXI Century Blood

First full-length studio album released. The title track's music video wins awards at film festivals. The band opens for The Killers in Monterrey.

2018
Queen of the Murder Scene

Second album — a 13-track concept album about obsession, duality, and madness. The band performs at Rock al Parque, Latin America's biggest rock festival.

2020
Signed with Lava Records

The Warning signs a multi-album deal with Lava / Republic Records. They begin working on their major-label debut with producer David Bendeth.

2022
ERROR

Third studio album Error released. Touring with Halestorm, The Pretty Reckless, Three Days Grace, and opening for Guns N' Roses in Monterrey. Single "Money" hits Billboard Mainstream Rock chart.

2023
Muse Tour & 10th Anniversary

Supports Muse on the Will of the People tour across Mexico and Europe — the band's first visit to Europe. Performs at the MTV Video Music Awards. Celebrates 10-year anniversary at the Pepsi Center, CDMX.

2024
Keep Me Fed & Latin Grammy Nomination

Fourth album Keep Me Fed drops June 28. The single "S!CK" hits #8 on Billboard Mainstream Rock. "Qué Más Quieres" earns a Latin Grammy nomination for Best Rock Song. European headline tour sells out all 19 stops. Performs at MTV Europe Music Awards.

2025
Auditorio Nacional & Concert Film

Sells out three nights at 10,000-capacity Auditorio Nacional in Mexico City. Releases first concert film Live from Auditorio Nacional, CDMX. Tours South America, Europe, and the US. Performs with Billy Idol at CBS's Grammy Celebration of Latin Music.

2026
New Music & Yungblud Tour

New singles "Kerosene" and "Ego" drop. Set to support Yungblud on a major UK and North American arena tour in spring/summer.

Sound

Hard Rock with Soul

The Warning's sound has been described as hard rock, alternative metal, post-grunge, and alternative rock — a fusion of heavy riffs, melodic hooks, and classically-trained musicianship. Their songs weave together English and Spanish lyrics, reflecting their Mexican heritage and global ambition.

🎸

Influences

Metallica, Muse, My Chemical Romance, Pink Floyd, Queen, Paramore, Badflower, and a wide range from classical to K-pop and J-rock.

🎤

Heroes

Hayley Williams (Paramore), Esperanza Spalding, Sheila E., Lzzy Hale (Halestorm) — female artists who've inspired the sisters.

🎹

Training

All three sisters trained classically on piano from early childhood, giving their music a sophisticated harmonic foundation beneath the hard rock exterior.
